\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{\arabic*.}, leftmargin=*, itemsep=0.75ex]
\item
% [Topics: logarithms, exponentials, modelling, differentiation] [Marks: 8] [Difficulty: 3]

A dose of a sedative is given to a patient.

The amount of the sedative, $x$ milligrams, in the patient's bloodstream $t$ hours after the dose was given, is found to satisfy the equation
\[
\log_{10} x = 2.58 - 0.071t
\]

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\alph*)}]
\item Show that this equation can be written in the form
\[
x = p\, q^{-t}
\]
where $p$ and $q$ are constants to be found. Give the value of $p$ to the nearest whole number and the value of $q$ to 2 significant figures.

\item With reference to the equation in part (a), interpret the value of the constant $p$.
\end{enumerate}

When a different dose of the sedative is given to another patient, the values of $x$ and $t$ satisfy the equation
\[
 x = 350 \times 1.3^{-t}
\]

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\alph*)}, resume]
\item Use calculus to find, to 2 significant figures, the value of $\dfrac{dx}{dt}$ when $t = 5$.
\end{enumerate}

\bigskip
\hrulefill
\bigskip

\item
% [Topics: trigonometry, trigonometric equations, trigonometric identities] [Marks: 8] [Difficulty: 3]

In this question you must show detailed reasoning.

Solutions relying entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\roman*)}]
\item Solve, for $0 < x \le \pi$, the equation
\[ 5 \sec^2 x - 3 \tan x = 7 \]
 giving the answers, as appropriate, to 3 significant figures.

\item Prove that
\[ \frac{\sin 3\varphi}{\sin \varphi} + \frac{\cos 3\varphi}{\cos \varphi} \equiv 4 \cos 2\varphi. \]
\end{enumerate}

\end{enumerate}
